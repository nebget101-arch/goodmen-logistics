1. Introduction

FleetNeuron AI (“FleetNeuron,” “we,” “our,” or “us”) provides software and services for fleet management, driver onboarding, compliance workflows, and related operations. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and protect personal information when you use our website, applications, and services (collectively, the “Services”).

By using the Services, you acknowledge that you have read this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Services.

2. Who this policy covers

This policy applies to:

  • Organization users: individuals who access the Services on behalf of a motor carrier, fleet operator, broker, or other business customer (“Customer”).
  • Drivers and applicants: individuals whose information is submitted or collected through driver applications, onboarding, safety, or compliance features connected to a Customer account.
  • Visitors: individuals who browse our marketing site or contact us without yet having a full account.

3. Our role and Customer responsibility

Where a Customer uses the Services to manage its workforce and operations, the Customer typically decides why and how certain driver or employee information is collected and may be legally considered the “controller” or “business” for that data. FleetNeuron processes such information on the Customer’s instructions to provide the Services, subject to our agreement with the Customer and applicable law.

If you are a driver or applicant and have questions about how a specific Customer uses your data, you should contact that organization directly. You may also contact us using the information below for access or other requests we can reasonably fulfill.

4. Information we collect

Depending on how you use the Services, we may collect:

4.1 Account and contact information

  • Name, work email, phone number, job title, and similar identifiers.
  • Login credentials and security-related information (e.g., session tokens, device identifiers).

4.2 Fleet and company data

  • Business identifiers such as company name, DOT number, address, billing contacts, and configuration you provide in the product.
  • Operational records you or your organization upload or enter (e.g., driver qualification files, safety events, documents, notes, and workflow status).

4.3 Driver and employment-related information

  • Information submitted in applications, onboarding packets, or compliance workflows, which may include CDL details, employment history, safety history, drug and alcohol program data where applicable, motor vehicle records (MVR) authorizations and results, and signatures or consents.
  • Communications metadata related to operational messages (e.g., delivery status of email or SMS), subject to your communication preferences.

4.4 Payment and billing information

  • When you purchase paid plans, payment details are collected and processed by our payment service provider(s). We typically receive limited information (such as the last four digits of a card, billing address, subscription status, and transaction identifiers)—not your full payment card number.
  • Invoices, tax identifiers, and billing history as needed for accounting and disputes.

4.5 Technical, usage, and security data

  • IP address, browser type, device type, approximate location derived from IP, and timestamps.
  • Product usage events, error logs, and diagnostics that help us operate, secure, and improve the Services.

4.6 Information from integrations and third parties

  • Data imported from or sent to third-party systems you or your Customer connect (e.g., background checks, MVR providers, telematics, HR systems), as authorized by the Customer.

5. How we use information

We use personal information to:

  • Provide, maintain, and secure the Services, including authentication and customer support.
  • Perform contracts with Customers and deliver features they configure (e.g., onboarding, DQF workflows, notifications).
  • Process payments, prevent fraud, and manage subscriptions, credits, and taxes as applicable.
  • Communicate by email and phone (SMS/voice) for operational, safety, compliance, and service messages, and—where permitted—for product updates or marketing, consistent with your choices.
  • Comply with law and industry obligations (including transportation safety and employment-related record-keeping where applicable).
  • Improve reliability and develop features, including automated and AI-assisted tools that may analyze or summarize content you or your organization submit. Outputs are provided to assist human decision-making and are not a substitute for professional legal or safety advice.

Where laws such as the GDPR apply, we rely on appropriate bases including: performance of a contract; legitimate interests that are not overridden by your rights (e.g., security, product improvement); consent where required (e.g., certain marketing or cookies); and legal obligation.
