## Implementation Summary

Research-only spike. Produced `docs/research/FN-1619-load-profitability.md` covering all six requested sections:

1. **Market-rate data sources** — provider matrix across DAT iQ Rateview, Truckstop Rate Insights, Greenscreens.ai, FreightWaves SONAR, plus DOE diesel and broker portals. Recommended **DAT primary, Truckstop fallback**, with DOE EIA as a free fuel-cost auxiliary. Greenscreens/SONAR deferred as they don't substitute for the rate number.

2. **Profitability calculator spec** — explicit field-by-field mapping from FN-502's *historical* cost model (`buildDirectLoadProfit` / `buildFullyLoadedProfit` in `backend/packages/goodmen-shared/routes/reports.js:2851-3090`) to a *forward* version that mirrors the same outputs from estimated inputs (distance × MPG × DOE diesel; per-mile or %-of-rate driver pay; period-cost prorate reuses FN-502's number). Two new outputs not in FN-502: `breakEvenRate` and `netProfitPerMile`. Edge cases for multi-stop, FSC split, reefer MPG, zero-loaded-mile lanes documented.

3. **AI negotiation handler design** — new `backend/microservices/ai-service/src/handlers/load-negotiation-handler.js` mirroring the FN-1437 pattern: cached system prompt (`cache_control: { type: 'ephemeral' }`), `temperature: 0`, `logAiInteraction` instrumentation, server-side validation that `walkAwayRate < expectedRate <= targetRate` before returning. Output schema: `{ targetRate, walkAwayRate, expectedRate, rateUnit, talkingPoints[], rationale, confidence, rateSourceAttribution }`. Rate-source attribution is composed server-side from DAT response metadata (not LLM-generated) to remove a hallucination vector.

4. **FE wireframe** — new `/loads/profitability-check` page, dispatcher-only RBAC, two-column layout (inputs left / three stacked result panels right: Profitability, Market Rate confidence band, AI Negotiation Guidance). "Update offer" loop recomputes calculator client-side without a fresh AI call. Empty/error states for no-DAT-data, AI-down, and bad input.

5. **Cost / capacity** — at ~50 calculator runs/day/tenant, Anthropic cost is in the noise (~$7.50/mo with prompt caching); DAT licensing dominates (estimated $75–$450/mo/tenant). Caching strategy: `lane_rate_cache` table with 6h business / 24h overnight TTL plus mutex on cache fill; weekly DOE diesel pull via cron in `fleetneuron-integrations-service`. Tenant config additions: `avg_mpg_by_equipment`, `avg_deadhead_pct`, `toll_rate_per_mile`, `profitability_check_enabled` flag. Recommend gating behind a paid plan tier.

6. **Open questions** — 12 questions for product covering DAT licensing terms, caching window vs TOS, plan tier, multi-stop scope, FSC split, broker-history threshold, walk-away math, dispatcher-feedback logging, and equipment-class taxonomy mapping. To be mirrored as a Jira comment.

A follow-up implementation-ticket sketch is at the end of the research doc (cache + DAT client, calculator endpoint, AI handler, FE page, QA, tenant settings UI, telemetry).

## Key Decisions
- **DAT iQ Rateview as primary, Truckstop as fallback.** Largest dataset, dispatchers already cite it, defensible in negotiation.
- **Calculator mirrors FN-502's column names and structure** so the dispatcher sees identical vocabulary on the prospective screen and the historical reports.
- **Separate AI handler** (not bolted onto `load-driver-match-handler.js`) to preserve prompt-cache hit rates — different prompts mean different cache keys.
- **Server-composed rate-source attribution** rather than LLM-generated, to eliminate a hallucination path.
- **Feature-flag + paid-tier gate** because DAT licensing cost is meaningful and dispatchers are the buyer persona.
- **v1 toll heuristic ($/mi) over a toll API.** Toll APIs are expensive; the heuristic is good-enough for v1 and the schema supports replacement.
